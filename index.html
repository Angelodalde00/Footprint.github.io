Commute Distance (one way) 1 km

Calculation of Total Weekly Distance

Daily Round Trip Distance:
1 km (to college) + 1 km (back home) = 2 km

Total Weekly Distance:
2 km/day × 7 days = 14 km

CO2 Emissions Calculation

Average CO2 Emissions per km: 0.08277 kg CO₂e

Total Weekly CO₂ Emissions Calculation:
Total CO₂e = Distance × Emissions per km/week
Total CO₂e = 14 km × 0.08277 kg CO₂e/km = 1.16 kg CO₂e

Conclusion and Reflection

In computing my weekly carbon footprint from the Northern Bukidnon State College through commuting, I estimated that I discharged 1.16 kg CO₂e. This figure shows that even though I sometimes take a motorbike for short distance travel, my daily travel has these effects.

Thinking of this, I wish to point out that even the smallest action is very important when it comes to climate change. My current means of commuting is effective, but the idea to consider environment best practices inspires me. For example, I could further reduce my emissions through configured travel such as walking to college or biking.

I can adopt eco-friendly travel practices, including walking or biking to campus whenever feasible, to lower my carbon emissions. Additionally, arranging daily carpools with classmates can greatly reduce the overall amount of CO₂ emissions that students produce. In addition to benefiting the environment, carpooling helps students become closer and more united.

This exercise has served as a real wake-up call to me personally, and it has encouraged me to do something to change my behavior towards the environment. We all need to be aware of how we travel and do it in a way that helps preserve our environment and convince others around us to emulate the same habits.
